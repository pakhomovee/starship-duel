Starship Duel

A hidden-information duel

Learn to hunt in the dark

Two ships fight across a network of star systems. It's 1v1, turn by turn, zero-sum — and your exact location is invisible to your rival unless something gives you away. Score the map, hunt the rival down, or outlast the collapse.

👁 Fog of war 🎯 3 ways to win 🛰 Unlockable kit 💥 A shrinking arena
01 · Objective

Three ways to win

You can't just hide — every path to victory means leaving cover at the right moment.

Domination

At the start of each turn you bank control points equal to your income — +1 per single-star system you own, +4 per binary. First to fill the CONTROL bar wins on points.

Elimination

A landed Fire is a raid: it steals points, captures the system, and costs the rival a life (they respawn hidden). Take all 3 lives and you win the hunt.

Collapse

The arena shrinks: systems go supernova from the outside in. Anyone caught on a dying star is destroyed — outlast a rival who couldn't evacuate in time.

02 · Economy

Anatomy of a turn

Ships alternate turns. Each turn is a small budget of actions fuelled by the territory you hold.

What you get

  • 2 actions every turn — plus any you banked with Overcharge.
  • Energy income at turn start from systems you own: +1 single-star, +4 binary.
  • Actions beyond the base 2 carry over; the base 2 do not — so spare tempo is never wasted.
  • The second mover starts with a small komi handicap so going first isn't an unfair edge.

What you spend it on

Actions come in three families — the gallery below shows each one in motion:

  • Move & hold — reposition and duck back under cloak (free).
  • Score & strike — Claim territory and Fire raids.
  • Tools & unlocks — Energy-priced recon, cloak, and permanent upgrades.

Crash your bot or run out of time and you forfeit.

04 · Mind games

Information is the real weapon

The whole duel is a bluff over where you are and what you're building.

Fog of war

You start cloaked. The rival only ever sees a “could-be-here” cloud — and the ownership map is fogged, so a Claim you make while deep-cloaked expands your empire invisibly. A well-placed Scan tears the fog away:

Kit counters

The three unlocks form a rock-paper-scissors — the read on your rival's kit tells you which one to buy:

Deep Cloak beats ▶ Scan & Scanners
Proximity Alert beats ▶ Deep Cloak
Jamming beats ▶ Proximity Alert

Energy caches sit on the map and grow in value — but you collect one only by starting your turn on it, which exposes you. Risk for reward.

05 · The clock

The collapse forces a finish

No stalling to a draw — the arena eats itself until the two of you have nowhere left to hide.

Read the warnings

From about turn 24, systems collapse from the outside in toward a random surviving “eye”. A system first destabilizes — a visible ⚠ countdown giving you a few turns to evacuate — then goes supernova. Anyone still standing on it is destroyed.

Both ships get squeezed together, so every game resolves and hiding forever isn't a plan.
