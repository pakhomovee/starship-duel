Starship Duel

How to play Starship Duel

Two ships duel across a network of star systems — hunt your rival down while staying hidden yourself. 1v1, alternating turns, zero-sum, hidden information: your exact location is invisible to your rival unless something reveals it.

Two ways to win

  • Knockout — Fire on the system your rival is actually in for an instant win.
  • Map control — each turn you bank control points equal to your income from the systems you own (single +1, binary +4). First to the target (shown as the CONTROL bar) wins on points. So you can't just hide: you have to come out, Claim territory, defend it, and contest your rival's — which is exactly what exposes you to a knockout. Scanning, cloaking and unlocks are how you tip that risk your way.

Get caught on a collapsing (supernova) system and you're destroyed; crash your bot and you forfeit.

A turn

  • You get 2 actions per turn (plus any you banked via Overcharge).
  • At turn start you earn Energy from systems you own: +1 per single-star, +4 per binary system.
  • Leftover actions beyond the base 2 carry over to next turn; the base 2 do not.

Staying hidden

You start cloaked. Certain actions expose you (reveal your exact system to the rival): Claim, collecting a cache, and entering a rival-claimed system. Hold to slip back under cloak. Your rival only ever sees a “could be here” set inferred from your last known position and how many moves you've had since.

Caches

Energy caches appear on systems and grow in value over time. You collect one only by starting your turn on it — which grants its Energy (or a banked Overcharge action) but exposes you. Binaries pay more but are the busiest, most contested systems.

The collapse

To keep games decisive, the field shrinks: from ~turn 24, systems collapse from the outside in toward a random surviving “eye”, roughly every few turns. A system first DESTABILIZES — shown with a ⚠ countdown giving you a few turns to evacuate — then goes supernova. Anyone still on it is destroyed, so both ships are forced together and every game resolves.

Reading the board

  • Orange = Player 1, blue = Player 2. A ship at half-opacity is cloaked.
  • Glowing rings mark systems you can jump to — click them to move.
  • Faint halos mark your rival's possible “could be here” systems.
  • Step advances one bot action; Auto runs continuously; Stop pauses.
